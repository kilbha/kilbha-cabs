About Lambasingi Tour
Tour Information

Often nicknamed the "Kashmir of Andhra Pradesh," Lambasingi is one of South India's rare places where the mercury can drop close to or even below zero. While actual snowfall is extremely rare, the early morning mist, icy dew, and bone-chilling cold create an almost alpine atmosphere — especially in winter. Nestled around 100 kilometers from Visakhapatnam, this serene hill station sits roughly 1,000 meters (or about 3,280 feet) above sea level. Winter temperatures here range from 0°C to 10°C, and during peak winter months like December and January, the chill intensifies, sometimes dipping into sub-zero territory. With cool breezes drifting in from the dense Chintapalli forests, the air remains fresh and crisp throughout the year. The village thrives on pepper and coffee cultivation, adding a rustic charm to its already stunning landscape. Whether it's the fog-kissed mornings or the peaceful plantation trails, Lambasingi offers a refreshing break from the tropical heat of Andhra.
